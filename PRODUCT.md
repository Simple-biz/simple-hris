# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Internal staff at an HR/payroll services company, using the app on a mix of
desktop and mobile. The sign-in surface specifically serves mixed and occasional
users: employees who log in infrequently (to check pay, file disputes, view
hours) and are not power users. They may not remember exactly how the system
expects them to authenticate.

## Product Purpose

A simple HRIS (human resources information system) covering payroll processing,
employee self-service, bonuses, disputes, onboarding/offboarding, and reporting
across several role-based portals (employee, manager, HR, CEO, payroll clerk,
contractor, admin). Success is staff completing their HR/payroll tasks quickly
and confidently, with money-related data presented clearly and without anxiety.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, and quietly competent. The interface handles people's pay and
personal data, so it should reassure rather than impress. Plain language, low
visual noise, no flashiness. Three words: calm, trustworthy, clear.

## Anti-references

Not a flashy fintech marketing page. No gradient-heavy hero treatments, no
loud color drenching, no playful gimmicks that undercut trust around money.

## Design Principles

- Reassure at every money or identity moment; reduce anxiety, never amplify it.
- Plain language over jargon; assume the user has forgotten how this works.
- Low cognitive load: one clear primary action per screen.
- Consistency across the role-based portals so patterns transfer.
- Earn trust through clarity and predictability, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Body text contrast >= 4.5:1, visible focus indicators, full
keyboard operability, and support for reduced motion. Light and dark themes
are both supported and must each meet contrast targets.
